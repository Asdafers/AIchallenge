# Product Brief: Methodic

## One-Line Pitch

An autonomous research operations agent that helps companies turn business decisions into methodologically grounded data-capture workflows, then deploys conversational research agents that collect cleaner, richer, and more complete participant data than static forms.

## Problem

Companies want insights, but insights depend on data quality. A large amount of business data still comes from surveys, intake forms, customer interviews, employee feedback, market research, onboarding flows, and post-event questionnaires.

These workflows are often:

- Tedious for participants.
- Repetitive for organizers.
- Poorly designed from a measurement perspective.
- Low-context and unable to clarify vague answers.
- Weak at adapting to participant expertise, language, or situation.
- Hard to turn into clean, structured, analysis-ready datasets.

The result is a false sense of insight: dashboards and reports that look precise but are built on inconsistent, shallow, or biased input data.

## Proposed Solution

Build a multi-agent system with two primary experiences.

### Organizer Experience

The organizer talks to an AI research designer about:

- Purpose of the study.
- Business goals.
- Target audience.
- Required decisions the data must support.
- Constraints, risks, compliance needs, and timelines.
- Desired confidence level and segmentation needs.

The organizer agent then prepares:

- A research brief.
- Data schema.
- Question pool.
- Sampling and segmentation rationale.
- Measurement and statistical considerations.
- Bias and quality-risk review.
- A highly visual review package for approval.

### Participant Experience

After approval, the system hands the plan to a pool of survey agents. Each survey agent conducts a fluid, adaptive conversation with a participant while preserving the approved measurement structure.

The participant agent should:

- Ask questions conversationally.
- Clarify ambiguous answers.
- Adapt phrasing to the participant.
- Keep the conversation engaging.
- Avoid leading or biasing the participant.
- Capture structured data and supporting verbatim context.
- Stop when enough high-quality data has been collected.

## Why This Is Agentic

The system does not merely generate a form. It plans the data-capture strategy, researches methodological grounding, creates a structured instrument, coordinates specialized agents, interacts dynamically with participants, validates responses, and returns analysis-ready data.

The clearest agentic proof is that Methodic can be asked for a business decision rather than a survey. It then pushes back on weak research design, uses tools to ground participant follow-ups, and returns evidence-linked data to the requester.

## Target Buyers

Initial B2B target:

- Head of Research Operations, customer insights, product research, or revenue operations at a B2B SaaS company.

Secondary targets:

- Product teams.
- Customer research teams.
- Customer success and voice-of-customer teams.
- Market research agencies.

## Demo Scenario

A B2B SaaS company wants to understand why mid-market enterprise deals are being lost or slipping.

The organizer agent:

1. Receives or confirms a request from a product, revenue, or sales insights owner.
2. Designs a mixed qualitative and quantitative data-capture plan.
3. Creates segments for champions, economic buyers, lost deals, slipping deals, and converted deals.
4. Builds a question pool with branching logic.
5. Explains the measurement rationale visually.
6. Pushes back if the planned sample cannot support the decision.

Survey agents then interview simulated participants and return:

- Structured responses.
- Confidence and completeness scores.
- Clarified themes.
- Clean data tables.
- Evidence snippets attached to each insight.
- Approved CRM or product-context references used during clarification.

## Success Measures

For the challenge demo, show measurable improvement over static surveys:

- Higher completion rate.
- Fewer unusable or ambiguous answers.
- Better coverage of required variables.
- Richer qualitative detail.
- Faster time from research objective to data-capture launch.
- More transparent link between business goal, question design, and final dataset.
